# Task Management

**See what needs doing - from your conversations.**

---

## What It Does

Zylch tracks tasks that emerge from your emails and meetings. Know what you committed to, what others owe you, and what's overdue.

---

## How It Works

### Automatic Task Detection

Zylch identifies tasks from your conversations:

- **"I'll send you the proposal tomorrow"** → Task: Send proposal to X (due tomorrow)
- **"Can you review this by Friday?"** → Task: Review X (due Friday)
- **"Let's follow up next week"** → Task: Follow up with X (next week)

### Task Types

**Your Commitments:**
Things you promised to do

**Waiting On:**
Things others promised you

**Follow-ups:**
People or topics to check back on

---

## How to Use It

### See Your Tasks

Ask naturally:
- "What tasks do I have?"
- "What's overdue?"
- "What did I promise this week?"

### Check Specific Commitments

- "What did I promise Marco?"
- "What am I waiting for from the team?"
- "Show me follow-ups for this week"

### In Your Briefing

Tasks appear in your `/sync` briefing:
```
PENDING TASKS:
1. Send proposal to Marco (due today)
2. Review contract from Elena (due Friday)
3. Follow up with TechCorp (overdue 2 days)
```

## Examples

### After a Meeting

**In your meeting notes:**
> "I'll send the updated pricing by end of week"

**Zylch tracks:**
> Task: Send updated pricing (due Friday)

### From Emails

**Email from Marco:**
> "Can you confirm the meeting time?"

**Zylch tracks:**
> Task: Reply to Marco re: meeting time

### Follow-up Reminders

**You said:**
> "Let me follow up with Sofia next week about the project"

**Next week, Zylch reminds:**
> "Time to follow up with Sofia about the project"

## Managing Tasks

### Complete a Task

- "Done with Marco's proposal"
- "I sent the contract"
- "Mark the Elena task complete"

### Reschedule

- "Push the proposal to next week"
- "I need more time on the review"

### Cancel

- "Cancel the follow-up with Giovanni"
- "No longer need to do that"

---

## Task Priority

Zylch prioritizes tasks by:
- **Due date** - Overdue first, then upcoming
- **Person importance** - VIP contacts' tasks rank higher
- **Context** - Active deals, important projects
- **Your patterns** - What you usually handle first

---

## Tips

- **Check daily** - Ask "what's due today?" each morning
- **Be specific** - "Send contract" vs "do the thing"
- **Use dates** - "by Friday" creates a due date
- **Review weekly** - Clean up completed and cancelled tasks
